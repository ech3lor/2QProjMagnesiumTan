What's in a Name: Defining a Pandemic

Before learning how to combat a pandemic, we must first define what a Pandemic is. Quite simply, a pandemic is a disease that affects a significantly large area of the world, usually across countries and even entire continents. The World Health Organization divides disease outbreaks into six phases:

  1. Animal virus with no humans infected
  2. Animal virus has infected a human
  3. Small localized cases in humans begin to occur
  4. Human infection at the community level
  5. Disease spreading across countries but still localized to one WHO region
  6. Infection at the community level in a country not in the WHO region mentioned in phase 5

The disease is only classified as a pandemic upon reaching stage 6.
